HELMET DETECTION AND NUMBER PLATE RECOGNITION IN REAL-TIME

Helmet detection and number plate recognition are two important features of modern-day surveillance systems. These features are particularly relevant in the realm of traffic management and law enforcement. With the advent of computer vision and machine learning technologies and computer vision algorithms, these features are being efficiently integrated into various surveillance systems to ensure better road safety and enforce traffic legislation. In recent years, the development and integration of computer vision algorithms have enabled advanced surveillance systems to detect whether motorcycle riders are wearing helmets and recognize number plates on vehicles from surveillance camera feeds. The main need for helmet detection and number plate recognition is to enhance road safety and enforce traffic laws and ensure people's security. By detecting whether motorcycle riders are wearing helmets, law enforcement personnel can efficiently enforce helmet laws and take appropriate action against those who violate these legislations. Keeping multiple road safety measures in mind, the incorporation of helmet detection systems into traffic cameras allows for real-time monitoring and detecting instances where riders are not wearing helmets. Various methods, including background subtraction and moving object detection, are used to detect the presence of motorcycle riders' helmets in surveillance footage. However, one of the main challenges in helmet detection is to differentiate between helmets and other round objects that may appear in the footage. On the other hand, number plate recognition is a critical component in ensuring road safety and enforcing traffic laws. By recognizing the number plates of vehicles in real-time, traffic modules can issue fines to drivers who violate traffic regulations such as over-speeding and parking violations.
